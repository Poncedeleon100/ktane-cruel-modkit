On the Subject of Timer Timings

“Okay, give a read on the module.” “There’s nothing.” “Okay… 6/3.” “No, there’s actually nothing.” “1/8.” “No… THERE IS NOTHING ON THE MODULE!” “9/2.”

Note: If your module contains any components, you are looking at the wrong section of this manual!

Press the "❖" button to activate the module. The timer on the module will begin displaying random digits from ØØØØØ-99999, changing every second. The number display will also give a number. Refer to the table below to find out what number to submit. The left-most digit represents A, and the right-most digit represents B.

Number DisplayRule
ØA + B = a prime number*
1A > amount of lit indicators, B ≤ amount of unlit indicators
2A / B = a whole number
3A and B = a multiple of the module count, excluding needies**
4The digital root of A + B is odd
5A or B matches a digit on the bomb timer
6B - Last digit of S# ≤ A
7B - A > the number of distinct ports modulo 10
8A + B ≥ sum of S# digits modulo 18
9A and B = the amount of lit or unlit indicators.

When the rule applies to the digits, press the "❖" button again to solve the module. Pressing the "❖" button when the numbers displayed are invalid will result in a strike.

*Ø in this module is considered a composite number.

**If the module count is over 1ØØ, press the "❖" button when A and B share parity with the last digit of the serial number.
